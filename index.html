Featured Project

BookEase — Appointment
Scheduling System

Project Overview

BookEase was a web-based appointment scheduling system designed to help professionals manage client bookings efficiently using free, open-source plugins. Built on WordPress and integrating tools including Booking Calendar and Contact Form 7, the platform enabled calendar-based appointment booking, automated email notifications, client information collection, and manual payment tracking — giving small professional practices a lightweight but functional scheduling solution.

The project ran over six months, beginning with a cross-functional team of 20 and consolidating to a core delivery team of 12 as participation shifted, roles were reshuffled across stages, and a smaller active group emerged over time. Throughout this period, I functioned as the de facto PMO Lead — owning governance, reporting, document control, and meeting discipline end to end. I also worked closely alongside the requirements process, tracking deliverables and maintaining documentation alignment as scope and team composition evolved.

📊

Stakeholder Reporting & Governance

Produced two weekly reporting outputs throughout the project: a structured Highlight Report covering achievements, upcoming activities, stage tolerance status, risks, issues, and lessons learnt — and a RAG Status dashboard summarising timeline, budget, milestone status, and path-to-green actions. Both were distributed ahead of stakeholder review cycles, keeping the team and Programme Office aligned on progress and decisions required.

RAID Log & Risk Management

Maintained the BookEase RAID log on a weekly basis throughout the project lifecycle, tracking risks, assumptions, issues, and dependencies across four dedicated tabs with a summary dashboard. Each entry was assessed, scored, assigned an owner, and given a mitigation plan with trend monitoring. The RAID log fed directly into the Highlight Report and RAG dashboard, forming a consistent governance loop across all reporting outputs.

Policy & Programme Work

Nigeria NSHDP II —
National Health Strategy

Prior to relocating to the UK, I worked as a Programme Support Officer to the Director of Policy and Programmes within the Monitoring and Evaluation Unit at the Federal Ministry of Health Nigeria, where I supported the review process for the National Strategic Health Development Plan II (NSHDP II) — Nigeria's national framework for health system strengthening across 15 priority areas.

My contribution was operational and coordination-focused. I ensured meeting participants received daily agendas, managed attendance and sign-in processes, and coordinated stationery and working materials across review sessions. I took meeting minutes, managed travel documentation, and supported document workflows — including the compilation and internal distribution of the NSHDP II draft across all 15 priority areas.

Working across a national strategic planning process at ministerial level gave me early exposure to large-scale programme governance, multi-stakeholder coordination, and the document management disciplines that now underpin my PMO practice.
